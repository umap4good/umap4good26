Workshop on Trustworthy Machine Learning

December 15, 2025 Vancouver, BC, Canada Full Day · 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM

This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners working at the intersection of machine learning reliability, fairness, robustness, and privacy. We aim to foster interdisciplinary dialogue on building AI systems that are safe, accountable, and aligned with human values.

Call for Papers

Submit Your Work

We invite submissions of original research on all aspects of trustworthy machine learning. We welcome both work-in-progress and mature research. Accepted papers will be presented as posters, with a selection invited for spotlight talks. Submissions will be double-blind peer reviewed.

Submission Guidelines

Papers must be submitted in PDF format using the official NeurIPS 2025 LaTeX template. Author names and affiliations must be omitted for double-blind review. Submissions that are concurrently under review at other venues are not accepted. There are no proceedings — accepted papers will be hosted on this website.

We also encourage the submission of non-archival extended abstracts (up to 2 pages) for recent work published elsewhere or work-in-progress that would benefit from community feedback.

Workshop papers are non-archival. Authors retain full copyright and may submit extended versions to journals or conferences.
